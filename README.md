## Game Description

Panic Mode – The Garden Circuit is a management-style game where the player must keep a greenhouse system stable by maintaining several plants at the same time. Each plant slowly loses resources, so the player must move around the system and provide water and light to keep the plants alive.

The challenge comes from managing multiple plants simultaneously. If the player does not react quickly enough, a plant can begin to die. As more plants require attention at the same time, the player experiences increasing pressure and urgency, creating a gameplay loop focused on multitasking and quick decision making.

The game also includes support mechanics to help the player manage the system. The **airflow mechanic** allows the player to stabilize a plant temporarily. When airflow is applied, that plant remains stable for a longer period of time, meaning the player does not need to maintain it immediately.

Another mechanic is **grounding**, which provides a short calming period where the player can recover from the intense pace of the game. During grounding, the player has a brief moment to reorganize and prepare for the next cycle of plant management.

The main objective of the game is to **keep all plants alive for the full duration of the level**. If too many plants die because the player cannot maintain them quickly enough, the system fails.

# Setup and Interaction Instructions

## Objective

Keep your **9 plants alive for 90 seconds**.

Plants gradually lose **water and light** over time.  
If **3 plants wilt**, the game ends.

## Controls

- **WASD / Arrow Keys** – Select plant beds
- **Mouse Click** – Select a plant bed directly
- **Q** – Water selected plant
- **E** – Provide light to selected plant
- **R** – Activate airflow stabilizer (cooldown ability)
- **Space** – Activate grounding ability
- **Esc / P** – Pause the game

## Gameplay

Players manage multiple plant beds simultaneously.

Each plant slowly drains resources:

- Water
- Light

If resources become too low, the plant will begin to wilt.

Players must quickly move between beds to stabilize the system before plants fail.

## Surges

During gameplay, **panic surges** occur. These short disruptions simulate panic disorder triggers.

During a surge:

- False alerts may appear on healthy plants
- Controls may become slightly delayed
- Visual clutter increases

Players must remain calm and continue managing the system until the surge passes.

## Uncertain Zones

After a surge, some plant beds may become marked as **uncertain zones**.

Tending to these beds raises system tension faster, but ignoring them may cause plants to deteriorate.

This forces players to make quick decisions under pressure.

## Grounding Mechanic

Press **Space** to activate grounding.

Grounding represents coping strategies used during panic attacks.

When successful:

- System tension decreases
- Resource drain slows temporarily
- False alerts are cleared

Grounding has a cooldown and must be used strategically.

## Goal

Maintain greenhouse balance by keeping plants healthy for the full **90 seconds** while managing increasing disruptions.

# Iteration Notes

## Post-Playtest

After peer playtesting, several improvements were made to improve clarity and learning progression.

1. **Reduced the difficulty of the opening phase** so players could learn the mechanics before panic surges begin.
2. **Simplified the instruction screen**, since testers reported that the original instructions were too long and difficult to read quickly.
3. **Improved clarity of player objectives**, making it easier to understand that the main goal is to keep plants alive by managing water and light levels.

These adjustments helped players understand the gameplay loop faster and reduced early confusion.

## Post-Showcase

During the mid-term showcase, instructors and TAs suggested several improvements that we plan to implement.

1. **Design surge mechanics around real panic disorder triggers**, such as increasing cognitive load and environmental distractions.
2. **Simplify the grounding mechanic**, ensuring it feels calming and intuitive rather than stressful.
3. **Reduce text on instruction screens and add visual icons**, allowing players to understand controls more quickly.
4. **Introduce clearer progression across levels**, allowing difficulty to ramp up more gradually.

These changes will strengthen the connection between gameplay mechanics and the experience of panic disorder while maintaining engaging gameplay.
